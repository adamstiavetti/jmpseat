# Landing Page Waitlist Plan

Working name note: "Deadhead Club" is a working name pending legal and trademark clearance. This plan does not claim legal or trademark clearance.

Product principle: Utility first. Community second. Social feed last.

Identity principle: Verified privately. Anonymous publicly. Accountable internally.

This is a documentation-only plan. It does not create a landing page, form, framework scaffold, app code, package files, deployment config, or implementation files.

## 1. Purpose

The landing page exists to validate demand before a full app build. It should test whether airline people understand and want the product wedge: verified aviation-worker utility for base intel, layover planning, anonymous crew talk, career tools, and crew-friendly perks.

The waitlist exists for private beta recruiting and user discovery. It should help identify the first focused beta community, find ambassador candidates, and learn what people need before app-code implementation.

The page must not overpromise unavailable features. It must not imply airline partnership, employer endorsement, official airline affiliation, legal clearance, or public launch readiness.

## 2. Landing Page Goal

Primary conversion goal:

- Get qualified aviation workers to join the private beta waitlist.

Secondary goals:

- Collect role, base, and airline/company interest.
- Identify the first beta community.
- Recruit ambassadors.
- Validate feature demand.
- Capture safety and privacy concerns.
- Identify people willing to do user interviews.
- Learn what existing tools and communities users rely on today.

> A verified aviation-worker utility community for base intel, layover tips, anonymous crew talk, career movement, and crew-friendly perks.

CTA button:

Short product description:

Verify privately, participate under a public handle or room-approved anonymous identity, and help build useful aviation-worker knowledge that is easier to find than group chats and safer than public forums.

Feature bullets:

Waitlist confirmation message:

Footer disclaimer:

> Deadhead Club is a working name pending legal/trademark clearance. Deadhead Club is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or officially connected to any airline unless explicitly stated. Do not submit passenger private information, confidential company documents, exact crew hotel details, live operations-sensitive information, or airport security procedures.

## 8. Verification Expectation Setting

Users should know before joining:

- Private beta may require verification.
- Verification is intended to keep the community aviation-worker focused.
- Work email verification may be available, but users should understand employer email systems may log incoming verification messages.
- Manual verification may be offered later or during beta under private handling rules.
- The public waitlist page should not request sensitive documents.
- The public waitlist page should not collect badge uploads.
- The waitlist does not guarantee beta access.
- Aspiring aviation workers may be handled later with limited access.

Suggested waitlist note:

> If invited to private beta, you may be asked to verify aviation affiliation. Do not upload IDs, badges, schedules, passenger information, or company documents here. Verification, if needed, will happen through a separate private process.

## 9. First-Base Beta Recruitment

The landing page should help choose the first beta base/community by collecting enough structured demand to see where early density exists.

Evaluate demand by:

- Base/airport concentration.
- Role diversity at the same base.
- Airline/company concentration.
- Ambassador candidates per base.
- Interview volunteers per base.
- Quality of open-ended pain responses.
- Referral clusters from trusted aviation workers.

Minimum signal before choosing first beta community:

- At least 25 waitlist signups tied to one base/airport.
- At least 5 potential ambassadors or high-signal contributors tied to that base.
- At least 4 roles represented at that base.
- At least 10 people at that base willing to interview or give feedback.
- At least 10 respondents naming concrete base or layover pain.

Recommended first-beta filter:

- Choose one base across multiple roles if it reaches the signal threshold.
- If no base reaches threshold, continue waitlist discovery instead of starting app build around a weak community.

## 10. Ambassador Funnel

Potential ambassadors should be identified from waitlist responses, referrals, and interviews.

Signals:

- Willing to be an ambassador.
- Gives specific, useful base or layover pain.
- Has trusted peers to invite.
- Represents an under-included role.
- Understands safety rules.
- Does not primarily ask for excluded features.

Ambassadors do:

- Seed useful posts.
- Invite trusted peers.
- Provide feedback.
- Flag safety concerns.
- Model good behavior.
- Help explain what base/role content is useful.

Ambassadors do not:

- Act as final moderators.
- Verify users alone.
- Represent airlines.
- Approve sensitive content.
- Access private identity or verification artifacts.
- Pressure meetups.
- Promote vendors without disclosure.

Ambassador follow-up flow:

1. User indicates ambassador interest.
2. Founder reviews role/base fit and response quality.
3. Founder does short interview.
4. Ambassador receives expectations and safety rules.
5. Ambassador gets limited invite privileges only after acceptance.

## 11. Feature Demand Survey

Short ranking survey:

Ask respondents to rank the following from most valuable to least valuable:

- Crew Rooms.
- Base Boards.
- Layover Boards.
- Jumpseat Brief.
- The Galley.
- NonRev Deals.
- Ready Room.
- Ramp Talk.
- Crew Rest.

Optional rating:

- "How useful would this be for you?" rated 1 to 5 for each feature.

Open-ended questions:

- What do you currently use instead?
- What sucks about those options?
- What would make this worth joining?
- What would make you not trust this?
- What base or layover question do you wish someone had already answered?
- What should Deadhead Club never allow?

Interpreting responses:

- Strong signal: specific examples of base, layover, career, commute, role, or safety pain.
- Weak signal: vague interest in "networking" or generic social feed.
- Risk signal: demand for dating/swiping, nearby crew tracking, flight loads, schedule scraping, exact hotels, or confidential docs.

## 12. Metrics and Success Criteria

Validation targets before app-code implementation:

- 150 total waitlist signups.
- 75 likely aviation-worker signups.
- 40 signups concentrated around one base/airport or tightly related community.
- 10 ambassador candidates.
- 20 interview volunteers.
- 20% or higher referral-source responses from trusted aviation peers.
- At least 30 respondents rank Base Boards, Layover Boards, or Crew Rooms in their top three.
- At least 15 respondents mention specific pain with Facebook groups, Reddit, group chats, scattered notes, or current crew tools.
- At least 10 respondents express privacy or verification concerns that can be addressed in product copy or onboarding.
- Email open rate target later: 45% or higher for beta update emails.
- Email click rate target later: 10% or higher for beta invite/action emails.

Qualitative pain intensity:

- High: respondent names a specific base, commute, layover, career, or safety problem and says they would contribute or join beta.
- Medium: respondent likes the concept but gives general examples.
- Low: respondent wants generic social, discounts only, or excluded features.

Do not start full app build solely because total signups are high. Start only when there is concentrated demand and credible beta participation.

## 16. Recommended Waitlist Tools

Simple options:

- Carrd.
- Framer.
- Webflow.
- Simple Next.js landing page later.
- Tally.
- Typeform.
- Google Form.
- ConvertKit.
- Beehiiv.
- Supabase later.

Best low-effort validation setup before custom code:

- Carrd or Framer for the landing page.
- Tally for the waitlist form.
- Airtable, Google Sheets, ConvertKit, or Beehiiv for lightweight list management.
- Manual review of responses for beta selection.

Why:

- Fast to launch.
- No custom app code.
- Easy to revise copy.
- Good enough to validate base/role demand.
- Avoids premature engineering before demand is clear.

Avoid for this phase:

- Custom app build.
- Supabase-backed waitlist implementation.
- Auth or verification implementation.
- Any upload flow.
- Any promise of automatic beta access.
