# Competitive Positioning

Brand note: jmpseat is the canonical product and app name. This document does not claim legal or trademark clearance for the name.

## Positioning Statement

jmpseat is a verified aviation-worker utility community for base intel, layover planning, anonymous crew talk, career movement, and crew-friendly perks.

It should not compete head-on with schedule apps or non-rev load tools in V1. Its wedge is verified aviation identity plus anonymous, accountable, aviation-specific utility.

## Direct and Adjacent Competitors

### Flight Crew View

Observed pattern:

- Schedule and FLICA-oriented utility.
- Offline schedule access.
- Crew assistant and legality calculations.
- Hotel/airport information.
- Crew chat and tracking/friend features.
- Subscription model.
- App-store reviews surface concern around airline login or scheduling-software access policies.

jmpseat response:

- Do not try to beat Flight Crew View at schedule/roster utility in V1.
- Avoid airline portal login and schedule scraping.
- Compete on verified discussion, base intel, layover knowledge, and trust.

### StaffTraveler

Observed pattern:

- Non-rev load request community.
- Airline employee eligibility verification.
- Travel tips and deals.

jmpseat response:

- Do not try to beat StaffTraveler at non-rev load requests in V1.
- Do not build flight-load request infrastructure.
- Use NonRev Deals as a supporting feature, not the core product loop.

### CrewLounge CONNECT

Observed pattern:

- Roster/calendar-first app.
- Roster sharing.
- Layover meetup features.
- Restaurants/discount discovery.
- Hotel room and pickup utilities.
- Privacy settings around sharing.

jmpseat response:

- Do not clone CrewLounge's roster/calendar-first model in V1.
- Treat roster/calendar integrations as Phase 4 only after trust is established.
- Build community knowledge without importing schedules.

### CrewVIP

Observed pattern:

- Large crew discount directory.
- Location-based offers.
- CrewConnect.
- AI layover planner.
- Business/partner dashboard positioning.

jmpseat response:

- Do not rely on CrewVIP-style deals alone as the primary wedge.
- Keep NonRev Deals useful but secondary to verified community utility.
- Add sponsored deals only with clear labeling and review.

### Newer Crew Community and Layover Apps

Observed pattern across YoFly Crew, CrewRoom, Creweaze, Air Crew Meet, Rendezvous, and similar apps:

- Crew-only or verified crew access.
- Layover chat and city discovery.
- Crew marketplace or crash pad concepts.
- Nearby crew, matching, or meetup features.
- Some social/dating-like mechanics.
- Some live alerts or location-oriented features.

jmpseat response:

- Validate demand for layover utility, crash pad discussion, and crew-only spaces.
- Avoid public nearby crew tracking in V1.
- Avoid dating/swiping behavior.
- Avoid live airport/security/operations-sensitive updates.
- Include broader airline roles beyond pilots and flight attendants.

### Facebook Groups

Observed pattern:

- Large informal airline, base, role, and crash pad groups.
- Strong community density.
- Inconsistent moderation.
- Fragmented knowledge retrieval.
- Limited verification consistency.

jmpseat response:

- Create structured Base Boards and Layover Boards.
- Enforce verification and room rules.
- Improve search and AI summarization.
- Preserve anonymity where useful.

### Reddit

Observed pattern:

- Pseudonymous public discussion.
- Useful practical posts.
- Non-worker participation and inconsistent verification.
- Community-specific moderation varies.

jmpseat response:

- Use verified aviation affiliation to improve signal.
- Keep public identity flexible.
- Build aviation-specific safety policy.

### Blind

Observed pattern:

- Verified professional community.
- Work email verification.
- Anonymous workplace discussion.
- Explicit policies against confidential company information.
- Privacy caveat that employer email logs may reveal verification-code requests.

jmpseat response:

- Borrow the verified-anonymous pattern.
- Add aviation-specific room types, trust tiers, and safety rules.
- Disclose work-email verification caveats.
- Do not become generic workplace gossip.

## The Wedge

jmpseat should differentiate through:

- Verified aviation identity.
- Anonymous public discussion.
- Internal accountability.
- Base intelligence.
- Layover Boards.
- AI summaries and Jumpseat Brief.
- Ready Room career tools.
- Trust and safety built for aviation risks.
- Crew-friendly marketplace context.
- Inclusion of flight, ground, ops, dispatch, scheduling, regional, new-hire, and commuter roles.

## What jmpseat Should Not Copy

- Schedule/roster-first positioning.
- Airline portal login or credential collection in V1.
- Non-rev load request marketplace.
- Deals-only positioning.
- Feed-first engagement loops.
- Public-by-default workplace drama.
- Dating/swiping mechanics.
- Public nearby crew maps.
- Exact crew hotel databases.
- Live operational or airport-security updates.

## Strategic Non-Goals for V1

- Do not be a schedule app.
- Do not be a non-rev load exchange.
- Do not be a dating app.
- Do not be a public crew-location tracker.
- Do not be a generic social network.
- Do not be an airline operations channel.
