# Monetization

Brand note: jmpseat is the canonical product and app name. This document does not claim legal or trademark clearance for the name.

## Monetization Principle

Revenue should reinforce utility and trust. jmpseat should not optimize early revenue in ways that make members feel watched, sold to, or pushed into generic feed engagement.

## Research-Backed Context

Competitor patterns suggest multiple monetization paths:

- Flight Crew View uses app subscriptions for schedule and crew utility.
- CrewVIP centers on crew discounts, local business visibility, and partner offers.
- StaffTraveler uses non-rev load request utility and travel deals.
- Newer layover/social apps point toward premium social, crash pad, marketplace, and crew discovery features.

jmpseat should not rely on one of those wedges alone. Its strongest revenue options should build on verified trust, utility, and crew-friendly context.

## Free Tier

The free tier should support the core network effect:

- Verified account access.
- Basic profile.
- Access to allowed Crew Rooms, Base Boards, and Layover Boards.
- Posting, commenting, saving, and searching within reasonable limits.
- Basic Jumpseat Brief usage.
- Basic NonRev Deals browsing.

Purpose:

- Build verified member density.
- Capture base and layover institutional knowledge.
- Make the product useful before monetization pressure increases.

## Premium Tier

Premium should add utility, not status games.

Possible premium features:

- Higher Jumpseat Brief limits.
- Saved AI briefs and planning notes.
- Advanced search and filters.
- Base comparison tools.
- Commuter planning aids.
- Ready Room career/interview tools.
- Career Copilot.
- Contract Explainer with disclaimers.
- Deal alerts.

Avoid:

- Paywalling basic safety information.
- Selling credibility.
- Selling public visibility boosts for ordinary members.
- Weakening anonymous participation for paid users.

## Sponsored Local Crew Deals

Sponsored deals can work if clearly labeled and useful.

Categories:

- Crew-friendly restaurants.
- Airport-area gyms.
- Transportation providers.
- Uniform services.
- Luggage repair.
- Sleep and wellness providers.
- Local tours or downtime services.

Requirements:

- Clear sponsored label.
- Terms and restrictions.
- Location relevance.
- Manual review in early phases.
- No vendor spam.

## Affiliate Revenue

Affiliate revenue can support practical purchases:

- Travel gear.
- Commuter tools.
- Sleep and wellness products.
- Career prep resources.
- Approved transportation or booking partners.

Rules:

- Disclose affiliate relationships.
- Do not let affiliate incentives distort safety, career, or workplace guidance.
- Do not recommend products that conflict with trust and privacy principles.

## Recruiting and Job Posts

Recruiting can become a meaningful later-stage revenue line.

Potential products:

- Paid aviation job posts.
- Featured hiring pages.
- Recruiter accounts with limited permissions.
- Ready Room employer Q&A with moderation.

Constraints:

- Recruiters should not access private member identity without consent.
- Anonymous discussion should remain protected.
- Job posts should be clearly separated from member discussion.

## Vendor Marketplace

A future marketplace can include vendors serving airline workers:

- Crash pads.
- Uniform services.
- Tax and financial professionals familiar with aviation workers.
- Commuter services.
- Local layover services.
- Career coaches.
- Transportation providers.

Marketplace rules:

- Vendor verification.
- Manual review before self-serve scale.
- Clear complaint and removal process.
- No scraping of member data by vendors.

## Crash Pad Listings

Featured crash pad listings can generate revenue, but this category is high risk.

Requirements:

- Manual review at launch.
- Scam and safety reporting.
- No public resident rosters.
- Careful location granularity.
- Clear pricing, rules, and contact boundaries.
- No unsafe meetup pressure.

## Why Display Ads Are Not Primary Early Revenue

Display ads should not be the main early revenue model because they:

- Push the product toward generic feed engagement.
- Can reduce trust around privacy-sensitive aviation discussion.
- Often deliver weak revenue before scale.
- May conflict with a professional utility brand.
- Can make members feel tracked rather than protected.

Early monetization should focus on premium utility, relevant deals, recruiting, affiliate revenue, and marketplace services.
